Direct → Indirect → Induced: The Multiplier Chain

Every job lost in trucking ripples outward. Input-output multipliers capture how automation in one industry contracts demand in others — and partially re-stimulates supply-chain sectors.

Impact Layer Pre-AI Jobs Post-AI Jobs Δ Jobs Δ Labor Income Δ Output
Direct 2,429,610 2,360,526 −69,084 −$7B $0
Indirect 2,056,333 2,097,555 +41,222 +$3B +$8B
Induced 2,100,059 2,075,691 −24,368 −$2B −$5B
Total 6,586,002 6,533,771 −52,231 −$5B $0

Key insight: Indirect effects are positive (+41K jobs, +$8B output) because automation in trucking raises demand in adjacent supply-chain industries — petroleum, vehicle repair, insurance. The induced effect (consumer spending) contracts because lower wages mean less household spending. The net: labor loses, but aggregate output is neutral.

The Output Paradox

Total output barely changes ($1.370T → $1.371T). Automation doesn't destroy economic value — it redistributes it from labor to capital. Firms producing the same output with fewer workers means: higher profit margins, lower prices, or both. The dashboard must expose this redistribution, not just the headline job number.

Section 04

Diff-in-Diff: The Causal Engine

The I-O model gives us correlational changes. To make causal claims, the dashboard's DiD view needs treatment and control groups, a policy event, and parallel pre-trends.

ATT = (Posttreated − Pretreated) − (Postcontrol − Precontrol)

Applied to our data: Treated = industries with high automation exposure (trucking, food service, retail). Control = industries with low exposure (legal, R&D, religious organizations). The policy event = a specific AI deployment date or regulatory change.

ATT = (2,493,239 − 2,560,367) − (563,771 − 553,564)
= −67,128 − +10,208 = −77,336

This estimate says automation causally eliminated ~77K trucking-equivalent jobs — the raw −67K understates the effect because couriers also benefited from displaced demand. The DiD view in the dashboard will let you pick any pair and see this calculation live.
